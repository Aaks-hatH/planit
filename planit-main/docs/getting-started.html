Getting Started

Your first event in five minutes

From creating an event to admitting your first guest — this guide walks through the complete setup flow.

On this page
Create an organiser account Create your first event Add guests Share invites Before your event On the day

Create an organiser account

Navigate to planitapp.onrender.com and click Get Started. You will be asked to provide a name and choose a password. This creates your organiser account.

Guests do not need accounts

Guests receive a personalised invite link. They never sign up or log in. Only organisers and staff have credentials.

Create your first event

  1. 1
    Open your dashboard

    After logging in you land on your event dashboard. Click New Event in the top right.

  2. 2
    Fill in event details

    Enter the event name, date, time, and location. These appear on every guest invite, so make them accurate. You can edit them later.

  3. 3
    Choose a subdomain (optional)

    If you want a clean URL like myevent.planitapp.onrender.com, set a subdomain. Leave it blank to use the default event ID URL.

  4. 4
    Save and enter your event space

    Hit Create Event. You will be taken directly into the event space where you can manage everything.

Add guests

There are three ways to add guests to your event.

Manually — one at a time

Open the Check-in page for your event, then click Add Guest in the top toolbar. Fill in the guest's name, email, role (Guest / VIP / Speaker), and group size. An invite code is generated automatically.

CSV bulk import

If you have a guest list ready, use Import CSV to upload a spreadsheet. Download the template first to ensure the format is correct. Required fields are guestName and guestEmail. All other fields are optional.

Email is required for duplicate detection

PlanIt uses email addresses to flag potential duplicates at check-in. PlanIt does not auto-send any emails — the email field is used for deduplication and optional manual sharing.

CSV template format

guestName,guestEmail,guestRole,adults,children,securityPin,notes
Jane Smith,jane@example.com,VIP,1,0,,VIP seating reserved
Bob Jones,bob@example.com,SPEAKER,1,0,,Keynote at 7pm
Alice Brown,alice@example.com,GUEST,2,1,,Nut allergy
ColumnRequiredNotes
guestNameYesFull name as shown on badge and boarding pass
guestEmailYesUsed for duplicate detection. Not displayed to guests.
guestRoleNoGUEST, VIP, or SPEAKER. Defaults to GUEST.
adultsNoNumber of adults in the group. Defaults to 1.
childrenNoNumber of children. Defaults to 0.
securityPinNoUp to 6 characters. Requires PIN at check-in if set.
notesNoShown to staff on the boarding pass review screen.

Share invites

Every guest has a unique invite link in the format /invite/ABC123. When you create or import a guest, you can share their invite from the create dialog or the guest list.

The invite page shows the guest their event details, QR code, and a wallet-card view. They do not need to install anything — it is a standard web page.

See Guest Invites for the full breakdown of what guests see and how to use the QR media endpoints.

Before your event

  • Add any staff accounts under Settings → Staff so your check-in team can log in.
  • If you have assigned seating, set up your seating map and assign guests to tables. See Seating Maps.
  • Review Security Settings to choose your anti-fraud level (Relaxed / Standard / Strict).
  • Print or download guest badges if needed via the /badge/CODE endpoint.

On the day

Open the Check-in page on the device you are using at the entrance. Staff log in with their username and PIN. Guests present their QR code. Staff tap Admit or Deny from the boarding-pass review screen.

The walkie-talkie button is always visible at the bottom of the check-in screen for instant voice communication between logged-in staff.

For more detail on the check-in flow, see Check-in System.
