Walkie-Talkie

Push-to-talk for your team

Instant voice communication between every logged-in staff member. No separate app, no phone numbers — just hold and talk.

What the walkie-talkie is

The walkie-talkie is a WebRTC push-to-talk voice channel built into the check-in page. It connects all logged-in staff and organiser sessions in the same event, allowing instant verbal communication without leaving the check-in interface.

It works like a traditional walkie-talkie: hold the button to transmit, release to stop. Only one person can transmit at a time.

There is nothing to install or configure. The feature is always available on the check-in page for any logged-in user.

How to use it

The walkie-talkie button appears as a fixed circle button at the bottom of the check-in screen. It is visible to all logged-in users — both staff and organisers. The demo below shows what a live session looks like — one person transmitting, others listening.

  1. 1
    Grant microphone permission

    The first time you tap the button, your browser will request microphone access. Tap Allow. Without microphone permission, you can still hear others but cannot transmit.

  2. 2
    Hold to talk

    Press and hold the button. While held, your microphone is active and all connected users hear you. The button pulses to indicate you are transmitting.

  3. 3
    Release to listen

    Release the button to stop transmitting. You return to receive mode and will hear anyone else who transmits.

Who can hear

All users currently logged in to the check-in page for the same event hear every transmission, regardless of their role. This includes the organiser and all staff accounts. Users who are not logged in, or who are on a different event, do not hear anything.

There is no private channel or direct messaging — the walkie-talkie is a single shared channel per event.

Requirements

  • A modern browser with WebRTC support (Chrome, Edge, Safari, Firefox)
  • Microphone permission granted for the site
  • An active internet connection (walkie-talkie does not function in offline mode)
  • The check-in page must be open and the user logged in
Browser tab must stay open

The walkie-talkie requires the check-in tab to remain open and active. If a staff member switches to another app on mobile, the connection may drop. Keep the screen on and the tab in the foreground during the event.

Tips for use at events

  • Use headphones — on a busy venue floor, headphones or an earpiece make transmissions much clearer than phone speakers.
  • Keep transmissions short — state your name and message briefly, then release. Long transmissions block others from responding.
  • Test before doors open — have each staff member test their mic before guests arrive so any permission issues are resolved with time to spare.
  • Combine with seating map — staff at the door can confirm a guest's table over the walkie-talkie to a colleague managing seating, without either of them needing to change screens.
