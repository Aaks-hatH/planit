Check-in System

Running check-in on the day

Opening the check-in page

The check-in page lives at /event/EVENT_ID/checkin or /e/SUBDOMAIN/checkin. Open this URL on the device(s) you are using at the entrance. It is optimised for both desktop and mobile.

Organisers are automatically logged in when they open the check-in page. Staff see a login screen first.

Staff accounts

Staff accounts let your entry team log in to the check-in page without having organiser access. Each staff account has a username and a numeric PIN (4–8 digits).

Creating staff accounts

  1. 1
    Open the check-in page as organiser

    Go to /event/EVENT_ID/checkin. You are automatically admitted as organiser.

  2. 2
    Open Settings

    Click the Settings button in the top toolbar.

  3. 3
    Go to the Staff tab

    Enter a username and PIN for the staff member, then click Create. Repeat for each member of your check-in team.

  4. 4
    Share credentials with your team

    Give each person their username and PIN. They use these to log in at the check-in page on their own device.

Staff sessions and the organiser token

If you test a staff login from the same browser as your organiser session, PlanIt saves your organiser session and restores it automatically when the staff account logs out. You will not lose organiser access.

Scanning QR codes

Tap the Scan QR button (or press S on a keyboard) to open the camera scanner. Point the camera at the guest's QR code. Scanning is automatic — no button press needed when a valid code is detected.

PlanIt uses the device's rear (environment) camera by default. If that is unavailable, it falls back to the front camera. Camera permission must be granted in the browser.

What the scanner reads

The scanner reads both full invite URLs (e.g. https://planitapp.onrender.com/invite/AB12CD34) and bare invite codes (AB12CD34). The code is extracted automatically regardless of format.

Boarding-pass review

After a successful scan, a boarding-pass overlay appears showing the full guest profile for staff to review before admitting:

  • Guest name and role badge
  • Group size (adults and children)
  • Trust score
  • Contact information (if stored)
  • Special notes
  • Table assignment (if set)
  • Security warnings or flags (shown prominently in amber)

Staff tap Admit Attendee or Deny Entry. There is no time pressure — the screen stays open until action is taken.

Admitting and denying

On Admit, the guest is marked as checked in. A full-screen green confirmation screen appears showing the guest name, group size, and table assignment. It auto-closes after five seconds, or staff can keep it open.

On Deny, a red denial screen appears with the reason. Both actions are logged in the activity log with a timestamp and the acting staff member's username.

Manual code entry

If a guest's QR code cannot be scanned — phone screen cracked, no data, low brightness — use the Manual button in the top toolbar to type the invite code directly. The same boarding-pass review flow applies.

You can also check in a guest directly from the guest list by clicking the Check In button next to their name, bypassing the scan step entirely.

Offline mode

PlanIt check-in works without an internet connection. When offline mode activates:

  • A cached copy of the guest list is used for lookup
  • Check-ins are queued locally
  • The offline indicator appears at the bottom of the screen with a count of pending sync items

When connectivity returns, the queued check-ins are automatically synced to the server. Any conflicts (e.g. a guest checked in on two devices) are surfaced as security alerts for review.

Cache must be seeded before going offline

The offline guest cache is populated when you first load the check-in page with connectivity. If you open the page for the first time offline, no cache will be available and lookups will fail. Load the page at the venue before losing connectivity.

Anti-fraud and security settings

PlanIt includes several layers of duplicate and fraud detection. Configure these from Settings → Security on the check-in page (organiser only).

Security presets

PresetUse caseBehaviour
RelaxedCasual events, open sessionsWarns on duplicates but allows entry. Minimal friction.
StandardMost eventsBlocks already-checked-in codes. Allows manager override.
StrictHigh-security, paid eventsHard blocks on duplicates, trust score minimums enforced, PIN required.

Trust score

Each guest has a trust score from 0–100. The score decreases when suspicious behaviour is detected (e.g. multiple failed scan attempts from different locations). In Strict mode, guests below the minimum score are blocked. In Standard mode, they receive a warning on the boarding pass.

Guest flags

Organisers can flag specific guests from the guest list. Flagged guests trigger a security alert on the boarding pass when scanned, prompting staff to verify more carefully before admitting.

Manager override

If a guest is denied (e.g. already checked in, blocked), a manager with organiser credentials can approve entry via the Manager Override button on the deny screen. The override is logged in the activity log with the reason and authorising manager's username.

Live statistics

The top of the check-in page shows five real-time counters:

  • Total Groups — number of invite records
  • Checked In — number of groups admitted
  • Total Admitted — actual headcount (sum of adults + children across admitted groups)
  • Adults Expected — total adults across all invites
  • Children — total children across all invites

A progress bar below the stats shows the percentage of groups checked in.

Activity log

The activity log (accessible from the Log button, organiser only) records every action taken in the check-in system: guest additions, edits, deletions, CSV imports, check-ins, overrides, and settings changes. Each entry shows the actor, their role, the target, and a timestamp.
