Walkie-Talkie

WebRTC push-to-talk voice communication for your entire check-in team. No separate app, no phone numbers, no setup — just hold and talk.

What it is

The walkie-talkie is a WebRTC push-to-talk voice channel built directly into the PlanIt check-in page. It connects every logged-in staff member and organiser in the same event, enabling instant vocal coordination without leaving the check-in interface.

It works exactly like a traditional radio walkie-talkie: hold the button to transmit, release to stop. Only one person can transmit at a time. Everyone else listens. The whole team hears every transmission.

There is nothing to install or configure. The feature is always present on the check-in page for any logged-in user.

How it works technically

The walkie-talkie uses WebRTC peer-to-peer audio with the PlanIt server acting as a signalling relay only. Audio data travels directly between browsers — it does not pass through or get recorded by the PlanIt server.

  1. 1
    Session joins the channel

    When any logged-in user opens the check-in page, they automatically join the walkie-talkie channel for that event. Their browser requests microphone access. The PlanIt server adds them to the event's WebRTC mesh.

  2. 2
    Signalling via Socket.IO

    When a user presses the transmit button, a wt:transmit-start signal is broadcast to all other connected sessions via Socket.IO. Each receiving browser establishes a direct WebRTC peer connection to the transmitter.

  3. 3
    Audio streams peer-to-peer

    The transmitting browser's microphone audio flows directly to each listener's browser over encrypted WebRTC data channels. Latency is typically 50–200ms on a good network — fast enough for real-time coordination.

  4. 4
    Transmission ends

    When the button is released, a wt:transmit-end signal is broadcast. All peer connections close gracefully. The channel returns to idle until the next transmission.

How to use it

The walkie-talkie button appears as a fixed circular button at the bottom-right corner of the check-in screen. It is visible to all logged-in users — both staff and organisers — on any device.

Who is on the channel

All of these sessions are automatically on the walkie-talkie channel for an event:

  • Any staff member logged in to the check-in page
  • The organiser, if they have the check-in page open

The channel is always event-scoped — there is no cross-contamination between events. Staff from a different event on the same platform never hear your transmissions.

Guests with only their invite link (/invite/CODE) are never on the walkie-talkie channel.

Audio quality tips

  • Use earbuds or a headset rather than phone speakers at the door — reduces ambient noise pickup and echo from crowd noise
  • Keep the button held until you finish speaking — the audio cuts off the moment you release, so don't let go mid-sentence
  • Speak close to the microphone — at a loud venue, cup your hand around the phone mic
  • Avoid speaker phone mode at busy entrances — the crowd noise bleeds into the mic and makes your transmission hard to understand for listeners
  • 4G/5G outperforms Wi-Fi at venues — hotel and conference Wi-Fi is often congested; mobile data typically gives better WebRTC performance

Privacy note

Audio transmitted via the walkie-talkie travels directly between browsers over encrypted WebRTC connections. The PlanIt server handles signalling only — it never receives, records, or stores audio data. Transmissions exist only in real time; there is no playback, no recording, and no log of what was said.
