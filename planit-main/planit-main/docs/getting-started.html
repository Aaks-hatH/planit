Getting Started

Your first event in five minutes

From creating an organiser account through to admitting your first guest — this guide covers the complete setup flow.

On this page

No credit card. No sign-up fees. Free forever.

PlanIt is completely free to use. There are no paid tiers, no per-event charges, and no feature paywalls. Create your organiser account in under a minute.

Step 1 — Create an organiser account

Navigate to planitapp.onrender.com and click Get Started. You will be asked to provide a display name and choose a password for your organiser account.

Guests never need accounts

Only organisers and staff have credentials. Guests receive a unique invite link and present their QR code at the door — no sign-up, no app download required.

Once you have an account you will land on your event dashboard. This is where all your events live. You can manage multiple events simultaneously from the same account.

Step 2 — Create your first event

  1. 1
    Click "New Event"

    From your dashboard, click the New Event button in the top-right corner. This opens the event creation form.

  2. 2
    Fill in the event details

    Enter your event name, date, time, timezone, and location. These details appear on every guest invite card and wallet pass, so make sure they are accurate. You can edit them later.

  3. 3
    Choose your event subdomain

    Set a custom subdomain (e.g. summer-gala) for a clean URL like summer-gala.planitapp.onrender.com. Subdomains must be 3–50 characters, lowercase letters, numbers, and hyphens only. Leave blank to use the default event ID URL.

  4. 4
    Set a guest capacity (optional)

    Enter the maximum number of guests if you want PlanIt to track against a capacity limit. Default is 100 if not specified. This is informational only — PlanIt will not automatically block check-ins at capacity.

  5. 5
    Password-protect your event (optional)

    Add an event password if you want an extra layer of access control. Staff will need to enter this password when logging in to the check-in page.

  6. 6
    Hit "Create Event"

    You are taken directly into the Event Space — the hub from which you control everything: guests, check-in, seating, tasks, and analytics.

Step 3 — Add guests

There are three ways to add guests to your event. Choose the method that fits your workflow.

Open the Check-in page for your event and click Add Guest in the toolbar. A dialog opens where you enter:

  • Guest name (required) — appears on their invite, badge, and boarding pass
  • Guest email (required) — used for duplicate detection, never shown to the guest
  • Role — GUEST (default), VIP, or SPEAKER
  • Group size — number of adults and children in the group
  • Security PIN (optional) — staff must verify this at check-in
  • Notes (optional) — shown to staff on the boarding pass (e.g. dietary needs, accessibility)

An invite code is generated automatically. You can copy the invite link directly from the dialog or use the email share button to open a pre-filled email draft.

For large guest lists, use Import CSV from the check-in toolbar. Download the template first to ensure correct column headers.

Email is required for duplicate detection

The duplicate-detection engine fingerprints guests by email. Without an email address, PlanIt cannot warn staff if the same person tries to check in twice using different invite codes. Always include the email column even if guests will not be emailed directly.

If you prefer, you can share the event's public invite page and let guests request entry. From the event space, copy the event's public URL and share it on social media, email, or your website. Guests who click the link can view the event details and submit an RSVP — though you still need to create their invite record for them to receive a QR code.

For formal events with a curated guest list, the manual or CSV method is recommended. The public invite link approach is better suited to open events or meetups.

  • Opening a pre-filled email using the email share button (you review and send it yourself — PlanIt does not auto-send emails)
  • Embedding the QR code in your own email template using the /qr/CODE image URL — see Integrations & QR

Step 5 — Set up your check-in team

Staff accounts let your entry team log in to the check-in page without having organiser access. Each staff member gets a username and a numeric PIN (4–8 digits).

  1. 1
    Open the check-in page as organiser

    Navigate to your event check-in URL. You are automatically admitted as organiser.

  2. 2
    Go to Settings → Staff tab

    Click Settings in the toolbar. Open the Staff tab. Enter a username and PIN for each team member, then click Create.

  3. 3
    Share credentials with your team

    Give each person their username and PIN. They visit the check-in URL on their own device, enter their credentials, and are in. Sessions persist for the duration of the event.

Pre-event checklist

Run through these steps before your event day to make sure everything is ready:

  • ✓ All guests added (manually or via CSV)
  • ✓ Invite links shared with guests
  • ✓ Staff accounts created and credentials distributed
  • ✓ Seating map built and guests assigned to tables (if using assigned seating)
  • ✓ Security settings reviewed — choose Relaxed, Standard, or Strict
  • ✓ Check-in page loaded on event devices to seed the offline cache
  • ✓ Test a scan with a real guest invite on your check-in device
  • ✓ Confirm walkie-talkie works between staff devices (microphone permission granted)
Load the check-in page before you lose connectivity

PlanIt's offline mode caches the guest list when the check-in page is first loaded with an internet connection. If you open the check-in page for the first time at the venue without connectivity, the cache will be empty and QR lookups will fail. Always load the page at a location with internet before arriving at the venue.

On the day

When guests arrive, the check-in flow is:

  1. 1
    Staff tap "Scan QR" (or press S)

    The camera opens. Point it at the guest's QR code. Detection is automatic — no tap needed when a valid code is found.

  2. 2
    Boarding-pass review screen appears

    Staff see the guest's full profile: name, role, group size, trust score, table, any flags, and any notes. They take a moment to verify identity if needed.

  3. 3
    Tap "Admit Attendee" or "Deny Entry"

    On admit: a green confirmation screen shows the guest's name, group count, and table. It auto-closes after 5 seconds. On deny: a red screen shows the reason, with an option for manager override.

Live stats update in real-time at the top of the check-in screen: total groups, checked in, total admitted headcount, adults expected, and children expected.

After your event

All event data is automatically deleted seven days after the event date. If you need to retain any records, export them before the deletion window closes:

  • Guest list — download from the check-in page guest list view
  • Check-in activity log — download from the organiser settings panel
  • Analytics data — screenshot or record stats from the analytics dashboard

After deletion, the event's subdomain and all associated data are permanently removed. There is no recovery option after deletion.
